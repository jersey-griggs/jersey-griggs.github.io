FAQs:

What are Tarot cards?

The Tarot consists of a 78-card deck, each featuring symbolic images that are reflective of the human condition. Every deck is divided between the Major Arcana, which represents the spiritual journey of life, and the Minor Arcana, which speaks to our joys, triumphs, trials, and tribulations. Similar to a deck of playing cards, the Tarot has four suits (Cups, Pentacles, Swords, and Wands) that are indicative of the four elements, and 16 Court Cards, which highlight different personality traits possessed by humans.

What is an Oracle card?

Similar to Tarot, Oracle cards contain a message that is helpful for introspection. Unlike Tarot, Oracle cards have fewer rules and each deck varies in its imagery and meaning. As a result, Oracle cards can be used in conjunction with Tarot to further enhance the reading’s message. If you would like a Bonus Oracle card for your reading, you’ll be able to choose from two different deck options, so you can use the imagery that resonates with you the most.

How does Tarot work?

Tarot is a mirror. Every time a card is pulled, it reflects back on whatever is going on inside of the querent. Sometimes, we get so caught up in the hubbub of life, it’s difficult to wade through the muck that is blocking our path. We’re often unsure of what to do or we worry about how things will turn out. But Tarot is unaffected by this muck — it clearly reflects our inner wisdom when we need it the most.

Can Tarot predict the future?

Since Tarot taps into our intuition, it does not predict the future. However, it does allow us to better see the path before us. Anytime a reading is performed, the card reflects the querent’s current energetic status. According to the law of attraction, you are the creator of your destiny. If you are not happy with the message in your reading, you have the ability to change your course.
